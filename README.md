# PHC-PULSE
Primary and Community Health Centers (PHC/CHC) in India manage medicine stock, patient load, bed capacity, and doctor attendance almost entirely on paper, with no real-time visibility for district health officials. This creates three compounding failures: l Stock-outs and waste happen simultaneously — one PHC runs out of a medicine while a nearby PHC has surplus expiring on the shelf, because no system connects them. l Ghost attendance — doctor absenteeism at PHCs is a widely documented issue, with no reliable digital check-in trail. l Reactive, not predictive — officials find out about a shortage or overcrowded ward only after it has already become a crisis.
